# 2026-05-03 — Xid 79 (GPU off the bus) during INT4 + DFlash GuideLLM sweep

## Summary

While running a GuideLLM concurrency + I/O-length sweep against the INT4 + DFlash N=8 champion config on the RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell, the GPU fell off the PCIe bus mid-benchmark. Recovery required a host reboot. Three of five scenarios (chat, rag, agentic) completed cleanly; the fourth (codegen) is what triggered the crash.

## Root cause

`Xid 79` is NVIDIA's "GPU has fallen off the bus" fault — the PCIe link from host to GPU went unresponsive mid-operation. It is one of the most severe Xid codes; the kernel itself flagged `Node Reboot Required` (Xid 154 with action `0x2`).

Xid 79 has three common upstream triggers:

1. **Power-delivery transient.** RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell is rated ~600 W TGP. Stacking the INT4 target + 2 B BF16 DFlash drafter under saturating concurrency drives a sustained dual-runner forward pass with a high peak-to-mean current ratio. A borderline PSU or 12 V rail drooping on a transient causes the card to brown-out and drop the PCIe link.
2. **Blackwell sm_120 kernel corner case.** The Marlin INT4 + speculative-decode verify path on nightly vLLM is documented as fragile under high concurrency (vllm-project/vllm#30135 and related). Some shape combinations have triggered illegal launches in community reports.
3. **PCIe slot / cable seating.** Less likely here since the GPU was stable for ~75 minutes prior; mechanical issues usually fault earlier.

The crash came specifically during `codegen` (4K prompt × 1.5K output), the scenario that combines short-prompt prefill with the **longest output** in the sweep — i.e. the most decode-side speculative-decoding cycles per request. With DFlash at N=8 each cycle is a dual-model launch (drafter forward + target verify on a 9-token candidate batch). At saturation that's the highest sustained kernel-launch rate of the sweep, which makes both #1 (power) and #2 (kernel fragility) more probable than under the longer-prompt scenarios that completed.

The earlier `agentic` scenario (16K × 800) finished cleanly. Its prefill-heavy character means decode pressure is much lower — consistent with codegen-shape stress being the trigger rather than absolute load.

## Mitigation applied

- Re-run uses `MAX_SECONDS=90` to reduce the dwell time at peak concurrency. Reduces — does not eliminate — the trigger condition.
- Failed `codegen/` directory was preserved as `codegen.failed-xid79/` rather than deleted, per the project memory rule about confirming before destructive actions on repo-tracked content.
- No GPU memory-utilisation reduction was applied yet. If the re-run also crashes, the next mitigation tier is `--gpu-memory-utilization 0.85` (down from 0.90) to leave more headroom against power transients.

## Open questions

- Is this reproducible on `codegen` shape, or was it a one-off? The re-run answers this.
- Does it also trigger on `FP8 + DFlash` (no quant), or is it specific to the stacked INT4 + DFlash path? We did not crash during the original FP8+DFlash GuideLLM sweep on 2026-04-24 — circumstantial evidence that the INT4 stack adds enough variance to reach the failure threshold.
- Should the system be load-tested with `nvidia-smi -q -l 1` capturing power/temperature during the next sweep, to discriminate "power transient" from "kernel bug"? `nvidia-gpu-exporter` is already running and feeding Grafana — its Prometheus metrics for the affected window are in `prometheus`'s retention.
