Both quants + MTP (qwen3_next_mtp N=1), dense 27B, single RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell (96 GB). Fixed-concurrency ladder [1, 4, 8, 16, 32] · guidellm --profile concurrent · 2026-07-01.

NVFP4 + MTP nvidia/Qwen3.6-27B-NVFP4 · vLLM 0.24 FP8 + MTP Qwen/Qwen3.6-27B-FP8 · vLLM 0.19

The one-line answer

It depends on output length, not on which quant looks faster single-stream. NVFP4+MTP wins every workload at low concurrency, but under parallel load the winner flips by scenario: FP8 wins short-output serving (chat, RAG Q&A) because batched decode turns compute-bound and NVFP4's group=16 dequant tax bites; NVFP4 wins long-output serving (codegen, agents) where decode stays bandwidth-bound and MTP compounds. The much-larger NVFP4 KV pool does not convert into a higher concurrency ceiling — compute saturates first.

NVFP4 reports ~2.1× the KV-cache ceiling (smaller weights + fp8 KV from the modelopt checkpoint). Yet in the sweeps it never reaches that ceiling: on rag, FP8 sustained higher concurrency (21 vs 15) and NVFP4's throughput actually fell at the top step. The bottleneck under load is compute, not KV capacity.

What we learned

  • NVFP4+MTP wins single-stream, every scenario. C=1 aggregate is 20–40% higher (e.g. codegen 102.8 vs 71.9). For 1–4 concurrent users, NVFP4+MTP is strictly better.
  • Output length is the deciding variable — not the quant, not KV. Short outputs (chat 300, rag 256) make batched decode compute-bound, where NVFP4's group=16 dequant tax dominates and FP8's W8A8 tensor cores pull ahead past a crossover around C≈5. Long outputs (codegen 1.5K, agentic 800) stay bandwidth-bound, where MTP + 4-bit weights keep NVFP4 in front throughout.
  • The KV-ceiling advantage is a mirage under these workloads. Compute saturates before the 39.8× KV pool is exhausted, so NVFP4's structural headroom doesn't convert into higher sustainable concurrency. It would matter for very long shared-prefix contexts where KV, not compute, is the wall.

Which should you run?

NVFP4 + MTP

Low concurrency (1–8), or any long-generation workload: coding agents, tool-use agents, document drafting. Also the better default single endpoint (smaller footprint, faster interactive replies). Best C=1 latency in every scenario.

FP8 + MTP

High-concurrency short-reply serving: chatbots and RAG Q&A at 10+ simultaneous users, where aggregate tokens/s is the KPI. FP8 delivers up to +62% (chat) / +34% (rag) peak throughput once the batch is compute-bound.
