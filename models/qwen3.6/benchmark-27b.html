Findings

MTP holds up under concurrency. Scaling stays positive through C=15 in chat — the speculative draft+verify overhead doesn't compound badly under load.

Bottleneck is prefill, not decode. Every long-prompt scenario (RAG, summarization, agentic) caps at 1–3 concurrent users. Decode bandwidth (the "60 tok/s ceiling") is rarely the limiting factor when prompts get long.

One RTX PRO 6000 can serve roughly: 15 chat users · 6 coders · 3 RAG users · 1 agentic agent.

Methodology

GuideLLM runs a sweep profile per scenario: a synchronous baseline (C=1), a saturating throughput probe (C=512 nominal), then 8 constant-rate strategies between them. Each strategy runs for --max-seconds 120 with 10 % warmup discarded. Per-rate stats: median (Mdn) and mean across the steady-state window.

Token counts per scenario are sampled from a normal distribution; min/max are clamped at mean ± 2 σ. Distributions match production traffic estimates from OpenRouter and Databricks.

What's Tuned

Two changes from the stock vLLM v0.19 defaults:

# Speculative decoding via Qwen3.6's native MTP draft heads
--speculative-config '{"method":"qwen3_next_mtp","num_speculative_tokens":1}'

# Enables interactive token streaming under load
--max-num-batched-tokens 16384

Other community-recommended Blackwell flags (--async-scheduling, --cuda-graph-capture-size, --compilation-config '{"level":3}') turned out to be either redundant (default-on in v0.19) or invalid (wrong field name). See models/qwen3.6/README.md for the full discussion.
