Concurrency curves — aggregate output tok/s vs measured concurrency

Each line is one scenario. X-axis is the actual achieved concurrency (mean active requests), not the nominal rate. Saturating "throughput" probes are off-scale and reported in the table below; the curves here include only the synchronous and constant-rate strategies.

Why the curves diverge

Chat and codegen are decode-bound: 1.4 K and 1.5 K-token outputs let DFlash's block-diffusion drafter pay back its 9-token verify pass many times. Agentic, RAG, and summarization are prefill-bound: most of each request is the chunked prefill of a 8–16 K prompt, where speculation has nothing to speculate over. Summarization in particular plateaus and even regresses at higher constant rates, because each new request adds queueing without producing extra decode work.

Saturation behaviour

The "throughput" strategy in the GuideLLM sweep ramps the offered load until the server refuses to keep up. The peak active concurrency it reaches reflects how request-bound the scenario is — long-prompt scenarios run into KV-cache and prefill limits much earlier than short-prompt ones.

Takeaways

What the sweep tells us:
  • Decode-heavy scenarios scale beautifully. Chat reaches 4.5× the C=1 throughput at saturation (450 OTPS at C ≈ 70). Codegen reaches 2.6× at C ≈ 12 — limited by the larger verify-pass batch from longer outputs.
  • Long-prompt scenarios are gated by prefill, not decode. RAG, agentic, and summarization barely benefit from concurrency — chunked prefill of 8–16 K prompts saturates the GPU before there's spare cycles for decode parallelism.
  • Summarization regresses under load. Constant-rate runs at offered C ≈ 1 produce lower OTPS than the synchronous baseline, because each request fills the prefill budget without unlocking extra decode tokens. Treat as a single-request workload.
  • Chat saturation reaches C ≈ 70. Short prompts let the scheduler stack many concurrent requests in the KV cache. The aggregate 450 OTPS at peak suggests this config could comfortably serve dozens of light-traffic chat users with sub-1 s TTFT each.
  • P95 OTPS is much higher than mean. Up to 1058 OTPS in chat — the steady-state batch is bursty, and individual measurement windows can hit much higher peaks. The mean is the safe operational planning number.

  --enable-prefix-caching
  --language-model-only
  --max-num-batched-tokens  16384
  --speculative-config '{"method":"dflash",
                         "model":"z-lab/Qwen3.6-27B-DFlash",
                         "num_speculative_tokens":8}'

  VLLM_ATTENTION_BACKEND      = FLASH_ATTN

  --profile      sweep        # synchronous + 8 constant-rate + saturating throughput
  --max-seconds  90 (codegen, summarization), 120 (chat, rag, agentic)
  --warmup       0.1          # discard first 10 % of each rate's window
